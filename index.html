Methodology

Everything here comes directly from public data. No estimates, no adjustments.

Data source

All figures are drawn from NYC Open Data dataset ex6k-ym48 — Claims Report — Underlying Settlements and Claims Filed Data — published by the New York City Comptroller’s Office. The dataset is refreshed roughly annually, with each Comptroller report covering the prior fiscal year.

Scope

This page shows only claims with claim_action = SETTLED and disposition_amount > 0. It does not include claims still pending, claims dismissed without payment, or judgments entered against the city after trial. Coverage spans fiscal years —.

Normalization

The source data mixes case conventions and abbreviation styles (for example, “Police Department” and “POLICE DEPARTMENT” appear as separate agency values). The fetch script collapses these into single buckets and rolls fine-grained claim type labels into broader categories. Original values are retained in agency_raw and type_raw fields in the downloaded JSON.

What “settled” means

A settlement is the city paying to resolve a claim. It is not an admission of liability or wrongdoing by any party. In some cases the city settles because it is cheaper than litigating; in others, it settles because the claim is meritorious.
